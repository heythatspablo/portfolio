Unblocking the Biometric Bottleneck: How we reduced verification time by 76% while navigating shifting compliance landscapes.

The Challenge

High Friction, High Stakes. Unfamiliar.id‚Äôs core product is identity verification. The "Selfie Capture" flow‚Äîwhere users prove they are human‚Äîwas failing.

  • The Metric: User verification took ~75 seconds, with a dropout rate of 18%.
  • The Constraint: UK biometric compliance laws were changing monthly, causing constant scope creep.
  • The Conflict: Designers wanted high-fidelity animations; Engineers were struggling with model latency on low-end devices.

The Operational Strategy

As the DPM, my job wasn't just to "manage tickets"‚Äîit was to unblock the flow of value.

1. Dual-Track Delivery (The "Legal" Fix)

Problem: Every time Legal changed a requirement, the entire sprint would freeze.
Solution: I restructured our Jira/Linear workflow into two distinct tracks:

Track A (Compliance/Backend)
Locked for 6-week cycles. No changes allowed.
Track B (UX/Frontend)
2-week agile sprints for UI polish and experimentation.

Result: Engineering could build the compliance infrastructure without waiting for the UI pixel-tweaks.

2. The "Lighting Resilience" Sprint (The "Tech" Fix)

Problem: The AI model failed in low light, causing users to fail verification.
Solution: I coordinated a cross-functional "Swarm" sprint. We paused feature work for 2 weeks.

  • Designers built "low-light" UI feedback loops (e.g., "Move to a brighter area").
  • ML Engineers retrained the model on darker datasets.

Result: Pass rates jumped from 82% to 94%.

3. Instituting Design QA (The "Process" Fix)

Problem: 40% of engineering tickets were "rework" caused by misinterpreting Figma files.
Solution: I introduced a mandatory "Design QA" column in our Kanban board. No ticket could merge to Master without a designer's sign-off in the staging environment (Vercel).
