The "Master Prompt": How I Generate Personas, Tone, and Copy in One Session

Most people use AI for one-off tasks. I built a "Master Prompt" workflow that chains context together‚Äîgenerating deep user personas, enforcing a strict brand voice, and outputting weeks of ad copy in a single, coherent stream.

In the world of growth marketing and product storytelling, context is currency.

If you ask ChatGPT to "write 5 Facebook ads for an identity verification app," you get generic, robotic fluff. Why? Because the AI doesn't know who it's talking to, or how your brand speaks.

At Unfamiliar.id, we needed to launch campaigns fast, but we couldn't sacrifice our specific "Empowering & Trustworthy" tone of voice.

I developed a technique I call the "Multi-Check-In Master Prompt."

It‚Äôs not just a prompt; it‚Äôs a session architecture. It forces the AI to build a "mental model" of our strategy before it writes a single word of copy. Here is the framework.

Phase 1: The Context Injection (The "Upload")


I never ask for output first. I start by feeding the "Brain."

> Prompt: "I am going to provide you with raw data about our product, Unfamiliar.id. Do not generate anything yet. Just acknowledge you have received it. Here is the Value Prop: [Paste Text]. Here is the Technical constraints: [Paste Text]."

Phase 2: Generating the "Synthetic Personas"


Once the AI understands the product, I ask it to hallucinate our users based on our real data.

> Prompt: "Based on the product data, generate 3 detailed User Personas.
> 1. The Stressed CTO: Cares about API uptime and security.
> 2. The Growth PM: Cares about conversion rates and onboarding speed.
> 3. The Legal Counsel: Cares about GDPR and compliance.
> For each, list their deepest fears, their daily frustrations, and the specific jargon they use."

This creates a reference layer. Now, when I ask for copy, I don't say "write an ad." I say, "Write to the Stressed CTO."

Phase 3: The "Style Guard" (Tone Calibration)


Next, I lock in the syntax using our Newsroom Playbook.

> Prompt: "Adopt the following Tone of Voice:
> * Empowering: Inspire action.
> * Informative: One idea per sentence. No fluff.
> * Trustworthy: No dramatic hype. Precision over persuasion.
> Confirm you understand this voice by rewriting this generic sentence: 'Our app is really good at checking IDs fast.'"

Phase 4: The Output (The Multi-Check-In)


Now‚Äîand only now‚Äîdo we generate the assets. Because the context is stacked, the output is aligned.

> Prompt: "Act as a Senior Copywriter. Draft 5 LinkedIn text ads targeting The Stressed CTO. Use the Trustworthy tone. Focus on the pain point of 'Integration Nightmares'. Structure the copy using the hook/problem/solution framework."

The Result


Instead of disjointed, generic AI copy, we get highly specific, persona-driven creative that sounds like us.

By treating the AI session as a "Project File" rather than a search bar, I can generate a month‚Äôs worth of consistent, high-leverage content in 20 minutes.
